# Engineering Thinking Resources

## Knowledge

- [Book: _Designing Data-Intensive Applications_ — Martin Kleppmann](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/designing-data-intensive-applications/9781491903063/)
  The definitive guide to understanding databases, distributed systems, and architectural trade-offs. Use for: learning about scalability, reliability, and data models.
- [Book: _Domain-Driven Design: Tackling Complexity in the Heart of Software_ — Eric Evans](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/domain-driven-design-tackling/0321125215/)
  The foundational text on domain modeling, ubiquitous language, and bounded contexts. Use for: domain modeling and architecture thinking.
- [Book: _Clean Architecture: A Craftsman's Guide to Software Structure and Design_ — Robert C. Martin](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/clean-architecture-a/9780134494272/)
  Focuses on design principles, component boundaries, and separating policy from detail. Use for: component design and architecture boundaries.
- [Article: "Architecture Decision Records" — Joel Parker Henderson](https://github.com/joelparkerhenderson/architecture-decision-record)
  Comprehensive guide and templates for documenting architectural choices. Use for: learning to write ADRs and design documents.
- [Guide: "Non-Functional Requirements" — Software Engineering Institute](https://www.sei.cmu.edu/)
  Frameworks for identifying and specifying quality attributes (performance, reliability, security). Use for: requirements and constraints analysis.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/softwarearchitecture](https://reddit.com/r/softwarearchitecture)
  A community dedicated to discussions on software design patterns, enterprise architecture, and system boundaries.
- [Software Engineering Stack Exchange](https://softwareengineering.stackexchange.com/)
  Q&A site for professionals, academics, and students of software engineering. Great for discussing conceptual design trade-offs.
